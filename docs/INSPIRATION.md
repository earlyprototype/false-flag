# Inspiration & Sources

FALSE FLAG is an independent, unofficial fan project. It exists because of other people's work, and this note credits it.

## The Wargame (Sky News)

FALSE FLAG's premise — a UK Prime Minister steering a live crisis triggered by a Russian false-flag attack, advised by a small team of experts under time pressure — is directly inspired by **[The Wargame](https://www.audible.co.uk/podcast/The-Wargame/B0FCLQ7W9B)**, a five-part series from **Sky News and Tortoise**, written and presented by Sky News security and defence editor **Deborah Haynes**. Real former ministers and military chiefs, including Sir Ben Wallace, Amber Rudd, and Jack Straw, play out the crisis in character.

This project is not affiliated with, endorsed by, or produced in association with Sky News, Tortoise, or any participant in the podcast.

Episodes that directly informed FALSE FLAG's structure and story beats:

- Episode 1 — *False Flag*
- Episode 2 — *Where's the War Book*
- Episode 3 — *Ultimatum*
- Episode 4 — *The Choice*

## Perun — "I Wargamed with NATO"

FALSE FLAG's engine design also draws on **Perun**'s YouTube video [*I Wargamed with NATO — Inside the Cross-Domain Command Wargame (2025)*](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ZFP0k7K9v1s) (21 October 2025), a walkthrough of a real NATO Allied Command Transformation Cross-Domain Command wargame — its scenario architecture, its Blue/Red/Grey Cell structure, and its adjudication approach. FALSE FLAG's phase structure, escalation ladders, and cross-domain effects (cyber/EW/space) owe a debt to that analysis.

## Wargaming design research

Beyond those two sources, FALSE FLAG's turn structure and adjudication model are grounded in the wider theory and history of professional wargaming: Kriegsspiel's Prussian origins, the Royal Navy's WATU convoy-tactics wargames (1942–45), Master Scenario Events List (MSEL) design, and NATO's Cross-Domain Command concept. The repo's own primer on this — [`Game_Mechanics/War_Game_Mechanics.md`](../Game_Mechanics/War_Game_Mechanics.md) — documents how these ideas map onto FALSE FLAG's phase loop (inject → deliberation → decision → adjudication → consequences).
